Century Classic - Appears in top 100 in 10+ decades across the entire period, never absent for more than one consecutive decade
Golden Age - Dominated (top 20) for 3-5 consecutive decades, then faded from top 100
Early Century - Top 100 from 1910s-1950s, largely disappeared by 1970s onwards
Mid-Century - Peak popularity 1940s-1970s, minimal presence before or after
Late Century - Emerged in top 100 during 1970s-1990s, maintaining or growing since
Modern Era - First entered top 100 in 2000s or later
Pendulum - Was popular in early decades (1910s-1930s), disappeared mid-century, returned to top 100 in recent decades (2000s+)
Lost Generation - Top 100 in early decades (pre-1950s), hasn't returned to top 100 since
Steady Decline - Started in top 100 in 1910s, gradually declining each decade, may or may not still rank
Steady Rise - Started outside or barely in top 100 in early decades, consistently climbing, now firmly established
Peak and Fade - Reached top 10 in at least one decade, then dropped out of top 100 entirely within 3-4 decades
Brief Moment - Appeared in top 100 for only 1-3 non-consecutive decades across the entire period
Resilient - Consistently appears in top 100 across 7-9 decades but never reaches top 20
Intermittent - Appears in top 100 for 4-6 decades but with significant gaps (missing 2+ consecutive decades)
Revolutionary - Sharp entry into top 100 coinciding with major cultural shifts (1960s counterculture, 1990s tech era, etc.)
